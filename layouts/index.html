§ 02 / Featured work

2021 UK Digital-First Census - one huge event, every ten years.

Case study
Client
Office for National Statistics
Dates
2018 – 2019
Role
Technical Lead / Architect
Scale
~1M concurrent users

The UK holds a census once a decade and 2021 was the first to be designed digital-first.

The challenge

The existing survey data collection platform was running on AWS. The architectural team had two mandates: prove that the system was cloud-portable by migrating it to Google Cloud Platform, and validate that it could handle the anticipated peak load - roughly one million households attempting to complete simultaneously on census day.

My role

I joined the ONS architecture team as a technical lead for the electronic questionnaire (eQ) application,. I subsequently moved into the Cloud Architecture team to work across software engineering, DevOps and architecture. I led the AWS-to-GCP cloud portability work, introducing Kubernetes-native deployments as part of the migration. I also designed the performance testing strategy — generating a million concurrent, realistic synthetic users is a non-trivial problem in itself, requiring careful modelling of submission behaviour and infrastructure headroom. This required close working with Google engineers to consider usage patterns that were beyond their usual planned levels.

The outcome

On 21 March 2021, the census launched and the digital collection system held. It handled a peak of 30,186 requests per second with 427,838 submissions during that peak hour. The groundwork laid during the architecture phase — cloud portability, Kubernetes orchestration, validated performance envelopes — proved robust under real-world load. It remains one of the largest public-sector digital delivery projects in UK government history.

A technical review of the delivery can be downloaded from the ONS: Delivering the Census 2021 digital service.
